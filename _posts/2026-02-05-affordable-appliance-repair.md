## Tips to Keep Your Appliances Running With Regular Maintenance

Routine [appliance maintenance](https://share.google/HWWaedBB431XZCLQd) is the easiest way to prevent expensive breakdowns. Taking a few minutes to care for a machine can add years to its life. Small steps now prevent stress later.

* Clean refrigerator coils twice a year. This stops overheating and keeps the unit efficient. Check door seals to make sure cold air stays inside.
* Inspect oven seals and wipe away food buildup around burners. This keeps cooking temperatures steady.
* Run a cleaning cycle in the dishwasher once a month. Use a cleaner that breaks down grease and scale. Clear the filter often to keep water draining well.
* Check water lines and hoses for cracks every few months. This is vital for any appliance connected to a water source.
* Keep machines level. Proper ventilation and balance prevent motor strain.

### How Often Should You Service Your Major Appliances?

Most large machines need a professional check-up once a year. Some might need more care depending on how often they are used. Refrigerators often perform better with maintenance every six months. Ovens and dishwashers usually do fine with an annual tune-up. Being proactive helps catch small issues before they become disasters. Waiting until a machine fails usually results in higher costs and more stress.

### Can You Handle Some Appliance Maintenance Yourself?

Homeowners can manage many tasks with a little effort. Cleaning filters, wiping coils, and checking for leaks are safe DIY steps. Always unplug the power and read the manual before starting. More complex problems need a professional. Issues with wiring, loud noises, or recurring errors require special tools. Do not risk personal safety or further damage by trying to fix complicated internal parts alone.
